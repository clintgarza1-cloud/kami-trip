A trip for Kami

Six ways to trade a costume weekend in New Orleans for something she'll actually remember. Three short getaways, three slow retreats where she paints all day and I work off Starlink — same money, just spent on beauty instead of a bar tab.

The idea

This replaces the Aug 8 Red Dress Run. Two shapes: a tight 3–4 night getaway, or a longer paint-all-day retreat where cheap-but-beautiful lodging frees up a daily delta — my spend stays flat, the savings cover her lost shifts, and the trip gets longer instead of pricier.

🛰️ The Starlink unlock

The retreats only work if I can work. Starlink Mini makes "remote" and "I have a job" stop fighting: ~$499 box, ~$50/mo (pausable), 10–15 min setup — unbox, point at open sky, plug in. Performance (100–200 Mbps) is a non-issue for everything I do. The only homework is picking a casita with sky exposure and bringing a small battery for outages. Two of the retreat lodges already run Starlink, so that risk is pre-solved.

My pick

San Cristóbal de las Casas is the smart execution — cheap-but-beautiful, open sky, fiber + Starlink redundancy, cool weather, and paintable on every corner. Lake Atitlán is the one she'd remember forever if we go a step further. Open each tab above for lodging, a day-by-day, and real photos. Then let her draw — and tell me the winner.

San Cristóbal de las Casas

Chiapas, Mexico

The smart retreat — cobblestone color, cool mountain air, something to paint on every corner.

✈️ 1 stop via MEX, ~6–7h🌡️ Mild ~70°F, afternoon rain🛏️ $73–124 / night🛰️ Town fiber + Starlink

This is the one I'd book. A colonial highland town at 7,200 feet where the air is cool, the streets are saturated with color, and Maya textile markets spill out by the cathedral. Cheap-but-beautiful lodging, real fiber and Starlink as backup, and a paintable subject down every lane. She works the morning light; you work the afternoon rain. Lowest-friction way to run the whole idea.

Lake Atitlán

Guatemala · San Marcos La Laguna

The dream version — a volcano-ringed lake built for slow living and remote work.

✈️ 1 stop to GUA + shuttle/boat🌡️ ~75°F, afternoon rain🛏️ $80–130 / night🛰️ Starlink advertised on-site

The painter's-retreat archetype on Earth: three volcanoes ringing a deep blue lake, indigenous color in every village, ~5,000 ft and cool, and an economy built entirely around slow-living remote workers. At $100/night you get a lakefront casita and change. The only cost is distance — it's Guatemala, a bigger door-to-door than Mexico. The reward is a place nothing else on this list can touch.

San José del Pacífico

Sierra Sur, Oaxaca, Mexico

The purist's hideout — cabins in the clouds, dirt cheap, gloriously remote.

✈️ Fly OAX + ~3h mountain drive🌡️ Cool & misty, 50s–60s🛏️ $50–105 / night🛰️ Starlink at Laberinto

A cloud-forest village strung along a mountain ridge, where the clouds roll in below you and pine mist swallows the road at dusk. Cabins are dirt cheap, the sunsets sit above the weather, and the silence is total. This is the romantic, off-grid extreme — and the only option where your connection is a genuine risk, so it lives or dies on picking the right cabin.

Oaxaca City

Oaxaca, Mexico

Best soul — Mexico's food-and-craft capital, cool and endlessly walkable.

✈️ 1 stop via DFW🌡️ Mild mid-70s, PM rain🛏️ $100–150 / night🛰️ Fiber + Starlink

The food-and-craft capital of Mexico, and the most soulful of the six. Mezcal, mole negro, hand-dyed rugs, ruins on a hilltop, and a Centro that photographs itself. The catch is the DFW connection — it eats a half-day each end, which is why this one wants 4 nights, not 3.

San Pancho

Nayarit, Mexico · Riviera Nayarit

The rest trip — a quiet beach town one street wide, Sayulita's mellow neighbor.

✈️ Nonstop AUS→PVR + 1h car🌡️ Hot & humid, low-90s🛏️ $95–150 / night🛰️ Town fiber + Starlink

One sandy street running down to a near-empty Pacific beach, with artsy Sayulita a 15-minute hop away for color and markets. Stay in calm San Pancho, day-trip into the noise, retreat to quiet at night. It's the pure-rest option — and the only one that won't beat the Texas heat.

Mexico City

Roma Norte / Condesa

Do-it-all — leafy, walkable, world-class food at 7,400 feet.

✈️ Nonstop Aeroméxico🌡️ Cool low-70s, PM rain🛏️ $110–150 / night🛰️ Strong fiber

A leafy, walkable metropolis where you land and you're there — no transfer. Tree-lined café neighborhoods, rooftop dinners, world-class museums and food, all at 7,400 ft of cool air. Honestly a city, not a reset — but it's the easy, do-it-all option that feels like a bigger, prettier version of your own walkable life.
